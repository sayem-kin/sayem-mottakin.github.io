Who am I?

I am a Machine Learning Engineer at Rokomari.com, Bangladesh. I received my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from North South University, Dhaka (regarded as the best private university in Bangladesh).

The field of my research encompasses a broad domain of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Software Engineering. I aim to build AI-driven intelligent software products, components to deal with crucial problems for both individual and community.

I have 3 years of experience in machine learning and extensive research experience in computational biophysics. At the Computational Physics Lab, our team introduced a human LRRK2 mutation into Drosophila, revealing key protein changes and potential Parkinson's treatments. I also developed an automated system for cellular toxicity detection to facilitate drug discovery. In addition, I built an application to forecast inventory needs, preventing overstock and understock. Currently, I am enhancing the features of the recommendation system. Besides work, I mentor underprivileged undergrads from Bangladesh who aim to become stellar researchers.

Did national holidays accelerate COVID-19 diffusion during the first phase of the pandemic in Bangladesh?

We employed a mathematical model and calculated the instantaneous reproduction number, Rt, of the 64 districts in Bangladesh to check the dynamics of COVID-19 diffusion. The comprehensive analysis shows a notable escalation of Rt value and thus the enhanced transmission rate in Dhaka and in all industrialized cities during the major events such as, garments reopening and religious holidays in Bangladesh.

Biomarker Discovery in Parkinson's Disease Using High-Resolution Quantitative Analysis of Mass Spectrometry Data

We explored the LRRK2 (R1441C) mutation in a Drosophila model of Parkinson’s Disease, finding it led to reduced movement, neuron degeneration, and shorter lifespans. The mutation increased kinase activity and phosphorylation of synaptic vesicle proteins like synaptojanin, suggesting new biomarkers and potential treatments for Parkinson's Disease.

Detection of Traffic offence using Computer Vision to Increase Road Safety

In this research, we developed a novel approach using state-of-the-art computer vision techniques to detect, classify, and track vehicles in real-time, while continuously monitoring traffic parameters for potential offenses. This method improves efficiency by consolidating multiple objectives into a single, streamlined model.
